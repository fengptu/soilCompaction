Module 1: Introduction to Agricultural Soil Mechanics

1.1 Overview

Agricultural soil mechanics focuses on how soil responds to external loads from tillage tools, tractor wheels, and field traffic. Equations related to soil density, porosity, moisture, strength, and stress are fundamental for predicting soil compaction in agricultural fields.

1.2 Three-Phase Soil System

Soil consists of solid particles, water, and air. The total soil volume is expressed as:

V = Vs + Vw + Va

V = total soil volume

Vs = volume of soil solids

Vw = volume of water in pores

Va = volume of air in pores

1.3 Soil Mass–Volume Relationships

1.3.1 Bulk Density (ρb)

ρb = Ms / V

ρb = bulk density of soil (g/cm³)

Ms = mass of dry soil solids (g)

V = total soil volume (cm³)

1.3.2 Particle Density (ρs)

ρs = Ms / Vs

ρs = particle density of soil solids (g/cm³)

Ms = mass of dry soil solids (g)

Vs = volume of soil solids (cm³)

1.3.3 Porosity (n)

n = 1 − (ρb / ρs)

n = porosity (fraction of soil volume that is pore space)

1.3.4 Void Ratio (e)

e = Vv / Vs

e = void ratio

Vv = volume of voids (Vw + Va)

Relationship between porosity and void ratio:

n = e / (1 + e)

1.4 Soil Moisture Content

1.4.1 Wet-Basis Moisture Content (MCwb)

MCwb = (Mw / (Mw + Ms)) × 100%

MCwb = moisture content on wet basis (%)

Mw = mass of water (g)

Ms = mass of dry soil solids (g)

1.4.2 Dry-Basis Moisture Content (MCdb)

MCdb = (Mw / Ms) × 100%

MCdb = moisture content on dry basis (%)

1.4.3 Volumetric Water Content (θ)

θ = Vw / V

θ = volumetric water content (fraction)

Relation to dry-basis moisture:

θ = MCdb × (ρb / ρw)

ρw = water density (~1 g/cm³)

1.5 Soil Stress Under Load

1.5.1 Normal Stress (σ)

σ = F / A

σ = normal stress on soil (Pa)

F = applied force (N)

A = contact area (m²)

1.5.2 Vertical Stress (Boussinesq Equation)

σz = (3P / (2π z²)) × [1 / (1 + (r/z)²)5/2]

σz = vertical stress at depth z (Pa)

P = point load (N)

z = depth from surface (m)

r = radial distance from load center (m)

1.6 Soil Strength

Mohr–Coulomb Criterion

τ = c + σ tan φ

τ = shear stress (Pa)

c = cohesion (Pa)

σ = normal stress (Pa)

φ = internal friction angle (degrees)

Cone Index (CI)

CI = F / Ac

CI = penetration resistance (Pa)

F = force to push cone (N)

Ac = area of cone base (m²)

1.7 Soil Bearing Capacity

qult = c Nc + σ' Nq + 0.5 γ B Nγ

qult = ultimate bearing capacity (Pa)

σ' = effective overburden pressure (Pa)

B = width of loaded area (m)

γ = unit weight of soil (N/m³)

Nc, Nq, Nγ = bearing capacity factors

1.8 Compaction Index

CIx = ((ρb,compacted − ρb,initial) / ρb,initial) × 100%

CIx = compaction index (%)

ρb,compacted = bulk density after compaction (g/cm³)

ρb,initial = initial bulk density (g/cm³)
