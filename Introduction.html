Module 1: Introduction to Agricultural Soil Mechanics

1.1 Overview

Agricultural soil mechanics focuses on how soil responds to external loads from tillage tools, tractor wheels, and field traffic. Equations related to soil density, porosity, moisture, strength, and stress are fundamental for predicting soil compaction in agricultural fields.

1.2 Three-Phase Soil System

Soil consists of solid particles, water, and air. The total soil volume is expressed as:

V = Vs + Vw + Va

1.3 Soil Mass–Volume Relationships

1.3.1 Bulk Density (ρb)

ρb = Ms / V

1.3.2 Particle Density (ρs)

ρs = Ms / Vs

1.3.3 Porosity (n)

n = 1 − (ρb / ρs)

1.3.4 Void Ratio (e)

e = Vv / Vs

Relationship between porosity and void ratio:

n = e / (1 + e)

1.4 Soil Moisture Content

1.4.1 Wet-Basis Moisture Content (MCwb)

MCwb = (Mw / (Mw + Ms)) × 100%

1.4.2 Dry-Basis Moisture Content (MCdb)

MCdb = (Mw / Ms) × 100%

1.4.3 Volumetric Water Content (θ)

θ = Vw / V

Relationship to dry-basis moisture:

θ = MCdb × (ρb / ρw)

1.5 Soil Stress Under Load

1.5.1 Normal Stress (σ)

σ = F / A

1.5.2 Contact Pressure (Pc)

Field contact pressure ≈ tire inflation pressure.

1.5.3 Vertical Stress (Boussinesq Equation)

σz = (3P / (2πz²)) × [1 / (1 + (r/z)²)5/2]

1.6 Soil Strength

1.6.1 Mohr–Coulomb Failure Criterion

τ = c + σ tan φ

1.6.2 Cone Index (Penetration Resistance)

CI = F / Ac

1.7 Soil Bearing Capacity

qult = cNc + σ' Nq + 0.5γ B Nγ

1.8 Compaction Index

CIx = ((ρb,compacted − ρb,initial) / ρb,initial) × 100%
