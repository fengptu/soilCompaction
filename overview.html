The primary goal is for students to acquire in-depth knowledge and understanding of soil compaction phenomena and new related technologies, enabling them to conduct advanced research and develop innovative solutions.

CLO 1: Analysis & Integration

Analyze and evaluate soil compaction phenomena by integrating the principles of **soil mechanics and soil dynamics** to identify complex problems with research potential.

CLO 2: Research Design & Modeling

Design and propose research approaches for identifying and assessing the diverse effects of soil compaction, and create **hypotheses or theoretical/computational models** to explain the phenomena.

CLO 3: Innovative Management

Develop or improve soil compaction management strategies by deeply considering the **relationship between machinery and soil compaction**, and evaluate potential impacts on crop production to create innovative methods.

CLO 4: Synthesis & Presentation

Synthesize and present work in the form of standard research papers/academic reports, linking soil compaction with **new technologies** and demonstrating the potential to create new knowledge.

ðŸ“š Content Summary (Course Description)

The course focuses on the theoretical and practical aspects of soil compaction in modern agriculture. Key areas covered include:

  • Agricultural soil mechanics and soil dynamics.
  • Identification, measurement, and effects of soil compaction on physical and biological properties.
  • The mechanical and dynamic relationship between farm machinery (tractors, implements) and soil compaction.
  • Impact analysis of soil compaction on crop production and yield potential.
  • Development and implementation of effective soil compaction management strategies.
